# **THE UNIFIED FRAME**

***The practice serves people who have been let down by the care they have been getting.***
***This is the unifying door. All marketing, content, channel strategy, and offer framing flows through this single frame. The patient may have been let down by conventional medicine, by functional medicine, by integrative medicine, by concierge primary care, by the wellness industry, or by any combination. The clinical state varies. The emotional history does not.***
***The patient walking into this practice has tried something. They have been told their labs are normal while feeling unwell. Or they have been sold supplements by someone who could not explain why. Or they have paid for concierge access only to find the same disease-oriented care delivered with better hospitality. Or they have been their own researcher for years and are tired of being the most thoughtful person in the room.***
***What unifies them is orientation, not symptom: they want more than they have been getting. They are willing to invest. They are looking for a physician who has time to think about their case.***

# **CHANNEL-SPECIFIC FRAMINGS**

***The unified frame is the public architecture. Channel-specific framings remain useful for tactical work where the audience is segmented:***

| **CHANNEL** | **FRAMING** |
| --- | --- |
| **Website (homepage, /start, /precision-call)** | Unified frame. The let-down patient who wants something better. |
| **LinkedIn organic, YouTube, longform content** | Mechanism-dense, peer-level. Speaks to the data-literate patient who has been doing their own research. |
| **Speaking circuit (Vistage, YPO, EO)** | Executive-adjacent. Less mechanism, more accessible. The C-suite professional who has read Attia and wants someone to actually act on it. |
| **Physician referral network** | Clinical complexity. The unsolved-case patient. Emphasizes metabolomics depth and synthesis bandwidth. |
| **Cold DM outreach** | The let-down patient. Pattern-matched to their visible signals (their content, their network, their professional context). |

# **CONTENT THEMES (NOT AVATARS)**

***Rather than building separate sub-avatar personas, content can be organized around themes that map to different aspects of the unified audience:***
- ***“Your labs are normal” content — for patients dismissed by conventional medicine***
- ***“Why your supplements aren't working” content — for patients who have been through functional or wellness medicine***
- ***“What conventional concierge medicine misses” content — for patients who have paid for access without depth***
- ***“What your body is telling you” content — proactive optimization for patients who are well but want better***

***These themes serve as content guides, not as audience segments. A single patient may resonate with multiple themes; the practice serves them all under the unified frame.***

# **THE PATIENT JOURNEY**

***Regardless of which channel or content theme attracted the patient, the journey through the practice is the same:***
1. They encounter the practice (homepage, content, referral, DM, talk)
2. They take one of two entry paths: Explore Your First Month ($500, fully refundable), or Schedule a Call (free 20 minutes)
3. They begin Concierge Precision Medicine — one ongoing physician relationship governed by the Intervention Accountability Standard
4. They continue at $500/month or stop after the first month

***The previous architecture's distinction between “Optimize” patients (motivated by performance) and “Heal” patients (motivated by relief) is no longer operative. Both populations are served by the same practice, the same product, and the same clinical methodology. The clinical work varies based on what the patient's biology presents — not based on a pre-imposed audience framework.***

# **INTERNAL CLINICAL STRUCTURE**

***The complex diagnostic case sub-track from the previous architecture remains internally meaningful. Some patients present with clinical complexity that warrants higher selectivity, bespoke engagement, and different operational handling. This distinction is internal only. Externally, all patients enter through the same single product.***
